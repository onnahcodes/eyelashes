Eyelashes Extension

Eyelash extensions are cosmetic applications that enhance the length, curl, fullness, and thickness of natural eyelashes. The extensions may be made from several materials including mink, silk, synthetic, human or horsehair. It is less maintenance than wearing mascara and fake eyelashes.The extensions can applied one at a time using a specially-formulated, semi-permanent glue that will not irritate or damage the natural lash. If you actually take care of them, it can last for six to eight weeks until they naturally fallout. Eyelash extensions aren't dangerous or bad as long as the technician is following the correct method and using the proper materials.

    There are three main types of eyelash extensions:
  • Mink lashes are natural fur that is obtained from minks, usually Siberian mink or Chinese mink.
  • Synthetic lashes are the most common type of false eyelashes, which are made from plastic man-made fibers.
  • Silk lashes. These are the mid-weight type of lash extensions, and are finer and more flexible than the synthetic type which makes their retention slightly better. Because of their light weight, silk lashes are good to use on older clients or clients with weak and flimsy natural lashes.

    Strip Lashes

    Strip lashes are a pre-made band of lashes that are applied with a removable adhesive. These are worn for a special occasion or during the day but are not to be slept in. Strip lashes are meant to be an enhancement that can get applied every day, but also must be removed every day. Strip lashes won't damage your natural eyelashes. If you learn how to apply and remove your them correctly.

    Strip lashes are removable and can be reapplied and worn numerous times before they are no longer good. It is are also referred to as false eyelashes, falsies, glue-on, or stick-on lashes. You won't have to visit a salon to get strip lashes to put on. You can apply it at home, remove them at home, and reapply them at home.

      Types of Strip lashes that win the market
  • Flared Lashes. consist of long-length fibers on the outer ends and short length fibers on the inner corner. They are most commonly used by makeup artists and also known as ‘cat eyelashes.’
  • Rounded Lashes are consistently round in style, consists of fibers that are round in shape throughout the lash. These lashes are more suitable for smaller eyes as they provide an enhanced eye look making them more awaked.
  • Voluminous Lashes newer and more innovative technique where multiple extensions are attached to one lash to give more dimension and texture. They provide a fuller look.
  • Stacking is the term used to combine a pair of eyelashes into one or customize/gluing the individual lashes over one another.
  • 3D lashes by definition means three-dimensional, which means these lashes are equipped with fuzzy layered ends that prevent them from laying flat immediately boosting the definition and dimension of your eyes. ... 3D lashes also hold a curl much better, allowing you to better shape your lashes and keeping them intact for longer.
  • Faux Mink lashes is premium type of synthetic lash that was intended to mimic the quality and fineness of real Mink Lashes. It's a vegan friendly option that is made from plastic fibres, although the technology has improved over the years to make them look more natural than before.
  • Crisscross are designed to create glamorous, feathery eyelashes. These lashes are made with varying lengths of wispy fibers that flare outwards in a criss-cross pattern for a voluminous and flirty, full-set of lashes.
  • Clusters are little sections of a strip lash or a group of lashes that are glued together in a cluster with a thick base. These can give you more of a natural look compared to strip lashes but are still very heavy to the eyes. These also should not be worn for more than a day and should not be slept in.
